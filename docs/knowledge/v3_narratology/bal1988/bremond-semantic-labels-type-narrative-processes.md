# Bremond's semantic labels type narrative processes

Source: Bal 1988, "Narratology", Ch. 3.2 Events (pp. 163-164)

Each process of improvement or deterioration has semantic content. Applying a semantic label to an event makes it possible to compare fabulas structurally across different texts.

## Improvement processes

- **Fulfilment of the task** — the subject completes what was needed
- **Intervention of allies** — help arrives
- **Elimination of the opponent** — the obstacle is removed
- **Negotiation** — a deal is struck
- **Attack** — forceful action toward the goal
- **Satisfaction** — can take the form of punishment, revenge, or reward

## Deterioration processes

- **Misstep** — a mistake, error, social blunder, moral lapse, or crime
- **Creation of an obligation** — the subject becomes bound
- **Sacrifice** — giving up something of value
- **Endured attack** — suffering an assault
- **Endured punishment** — suffering consequences

Each label can be further specified. "Satisfaction" splits into punishment, revenge, reward. "Misstep" splits into mistake, error, social blunder, moral lapse, crime.

## The initial situation

The initial situation of a fabula is always a **state of deficiency** in which one or more actors want to introduce changes. The development of the fabula reveals whether the process involves improvement or deterioration *with regard to the initial situation*.

## What this means for tvplotlines

These labels are more informative than our current "escalation" or "turning_point." Instead of "this event escalates the plotline," we could say "this event is an *endured attack* in a deterioration process" or "this event is a *negotiation* in an improvement process." The vocabulary maps well to TV drama: alliances, betrayals, sacrifices, missteps, attacks — these are the stuff of serialized television.

Question: is this vocabulary universal enough for TV, or too tied to folk/literary narrative?
