# Confrontation type (verbal/mental/bodily) reveals plotline nature

Source: Bal 1988, "Narratology", Ch. 3.2 Events (p. 164)

Events can be classified by the **nature of the confrontation** between actors: verbal (spoken), mental (thoughts, feelings, observations), or bodily (physical contact). Whether these contacts succeed, fail, or are indeterminate gives insight into the fabula's themes.

Bal's insight: "If the contact between the two most important actors is predominantly mental and unsuccessful, we may surmise that the theme is alienation." The *type* of confrontation, combined with its success or failure, points to thematic content without needing explicit thematic analysis.

## What this means for tvplotlines

This maps suggestively to our plotline:nature classification (plot-led / character-led / theme-led):

- Predominantly **bodily/action** confrontations → plot-led (external obstacles, physical conflict)
- Predominantly **mental** confrontations → character-led (internal conflict, alienation, desire)
- Mixed **verbal** confrontations across many actors → theme-led (institutional dynamics, systemic problems)

If events already have confrontation types, plotline:nature could be *derived* rather than manually assigned. This would be a validation mechanism: if pass1 labels a plotline as "character-led" but its events are all bodily confrontations, something is off.
