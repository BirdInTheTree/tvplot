# Fabula is a memory trace, not the author's plan

Source: Bal 1988, "Narratology", Introduction (p. 9)

"The fabula is really the result of the mental activity of reading; it is the interpretation by the reader, influenced both by the initial encounter with the text and by the manipulations of the story."

We never access the fabula directly — we reconstruct it. Writers mostly work alone; filmmakers are part of a team. How they proceed "we simply cannot know." What we can study is the text and the conditions under which it produces particular effects.

## Why this matters for tvplotlines

We work with synopses — plot summaries written by someone who watched the episode. These are already *reconstructed fabulas*, not texts. This is actually an advantage: our input is closer to the fabula level than raw scripts would be. But it also means we've already lost text-level and most story-level information (focalization, pacing, visual style) before our pipeline even starts.

The pipeline shouldn't pretend it's analyzing "the show." It's analyzing the fabula as reconstructed in synopses.
