# The protagonist is not free — they are constrained by the relational structure

Source: Moretti 2011, "Network Theory, Plot Analysis"

Moretti's analysis of Bao-yu in *The Story of the Stone* reveals a counter-intuitive property of protagonists: the protagonist is "not free" precisely because they are the most connected. Each chapter reshuffles Bao-yu's connections — he speaks to 54 different characters in the first 20 chapters — but "not once does the same group reform around him."

The protagonist has "a duty *towards the structure*: towards the relation-based society he is part of." This is unlike the Bildungsroman protagonist (Elizabeth Bennet) who is free to shape her own life. The network-constrained protagonist must maintain the connective tissue of the social world.

This suggests two different protagonist types visible through network analysis:
1. **Hub protagonist** — maintains network cohesion, constrained by connections (Bao-yu, Hamlet)
2. **Journey protagonist** — moves through the network, transforming connections (Bennet)

These correspond to different genres and cultural traditions: the relational Chinese novel vs. the individualist Western Bildungsroman.
