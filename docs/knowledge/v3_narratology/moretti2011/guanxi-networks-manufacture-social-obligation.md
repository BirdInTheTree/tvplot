# Guanxi networks manufacture social obligation through chains of transactions

Source: Moretti 2011, "Network Theory, Plot Analysis"

In analyzing *The Story of the Stone*, Moretti encounters a network pattern that doesn't exist in Western novels: **guanxi** — "connections" that are "neither individual nor society-based, but relation-based." These connections are artifacts: "manufacturing obligation," "chain of transactions," "indebtedness."

The key example (ch. 24): Jia Yun needs work → asks Bu Shi-ren for help → Bu refuses → Jia Yun meets Ni Er (a drunk/racketeer) → Ni Er lends money → Jia Yun buys a present for Xi-feng → Xi-feng gives him a job. A whole chain of transactions that manufactures obligation and creates asymmetry.

This matters for network analysis because:
1. **Edge semantics differ across cultures** — in Shakespeare, an edge means "these characters spoke." In *The Story of the Stone*, edges can mean reciprocity, obligation, transaction, debt.
2. **Local asymmetry + global balance** — individual guanxi chains are asymmetric (someone gains, someone gives), but over time they tend toward reciprocity. At the chapter scale, this produces asymmetry in chapters but symmetry across the novel.

## Implications for tvplotlines

- Different narrative traditions structure character relationships differently
- Edge types (cooperation, conflict, obligation, hierarchy) carry semantic weight that simple co-occurrence networks miss
- TV series from different cultural traditions may need different analytical frameworks
