# Moretti used network theory as visualization, not as a full analytical framework

Source: Moretti 2011, "Network Theory, Plot Analysis" (Epilogue)

Moretti's crucial self-assessment in the epilogue: he didn't actually need network *theory* — he needed network *visualization*. "What I took from network theory were less concepts than visualization: the possibility of extracting characters and interactions from a dramatic structure, and turning them into a set of signs that I could see at a glance."

He compares this to his earlier use of cartography in *Atlas of the European Novel*: arranging literary data spatially as "a principle of order — but not a full conceptual architecture."

Key admissions:
- The "region of death," "spaces of legitimacy," "symmetry" — none of these had much to do with network theory proper
- The study drifted from quantification to qualitative analysis almost immediately
- Edge weight and direction were missing, making the networks incomplete models
- The networks were all built by hand, not computationally

His conclusion: intuitive visualization reached a dead end. The next step required moving from images to **concepts** (network size, density, clustering, betweenness) and statistical analysis — the work Rhiannon Lewis continued.

## Why this matters

This is an honest account of the gap between borrowing a visualization technique from another discipline vs. actually using that discipline's analytical toolkit. For tvplotlines, the lesson is: don't stop at pretty graphs. Use the actual metrics.
