# Ideological gradation organizes event sequences

Source: Todorov 1971, "The 2 Principles of Narrative" (pp. 43-44), from analysis of Les Liaisons Dangereuses and Notes from the Underground

Beyond succession and transformation, Todorov identifies a third organizing principle: **ideological organization** — events are arranged according to a value system, typically as a **gradation** from lesser to greater.

In Dostoevsky's *Notes from the Underground*: the narrator is humiliated by Zverkov, then humiliates Lisa, then is humiliated again by his servant Apollo, and humiliates Lisa again "with still more severity." The narrative pattern is escalation through repetition — each cycle more extreme.

In Laclos's *Les Liaisons Dangereuses*: "the principle of succession and the principle of transformation are equally necessary to narrative. If we had to oppose narrative and poetry (cf. épos and lyric), we would have done so... on the basis of the transformative or iterative character of the text."

The **gradation principle**: events of the same type are ordered by severity, creating a pattern of escalation that is ideologically motivated. The pattern is broken "by the enunciation of a different ideology" — when a different value system interrupts the escalation.

## The two laws of narrative (from Les Liaisons Dangereuses)

Todorov extracts two "laws":
1. **Escalation law**: "If the repetition of a requirement increases, the negative results increase as well" — each attempt makes things worse
2. **Desire law** (from Adolphe): "the tragic nature of this situation results from the fact that, although obedient to the first principle [escalation], the protagonist does not therefore cease to desire" — the character can't stop wanting even when pursuit causes deterioration

## What this means for tvplotlines

This gives theoretical grounding to our "escalation" function — but with more precision. Escalation isn't just "stakes get higher." It's a **gradation principle**: events of the same type repeat with increasing severity.

The desire law is particularly relevant for character-led plotlines (Breaking Bad, Succession): the character *knows* things are getting worse but can't stop. This is a specific pattern — not just "escalation" but "escalation despite recognition of deterioration." It could be a subcategory of our functions: `escalation_despite_recognition` or simply noted as a pattern in pass3 structural review.

The ideological principle also explains why some plotlines feel like they have a "theme" beyond their events: the arrangement of events itself embodies a worldview (e.g., "power corrupts" = gradation of corruption events).
